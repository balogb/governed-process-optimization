# Example leadership scorecard (synthetic)

The same `process-kpi-log` data rolls up into a one-line-per-process view for stakeholders and
leadership. It is the at-a-glance companion to the granular, auditable log: each row carries the
baseline, where the process is now, the target, adoption, governance status, ownership, and a
**recommended action driven by adoption evidence rather than opinion**.

All processes, numbers, and names below are synthetic and for demonstration only.

| Process | Baseline | Current | Target | Adoption | Governance | Owner | Recommended action |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Application access provisioning | 6.0 days | 2.3 days | 2.0 days | 91% | Reviewed | Assigned | **Scale** - templated, two areas already reused it |
| Vendor onboarding | 12 days | 6 days | 5 days | 40% | Reviewed | Assigned | **Coach** - performance moved but adoption is low; re-sell the "why" to the owning team |
| Quarterly manual reconciliation | 4 days | 4 days | 2 days | 12% | Reviewed | Unassigned | **Sunset** - not adopted, not reusable; retire cleanly and redirect effort |

## How to read it

- **Scale** - high adoption plus a reusable pattern: template it and take it enterprise-wide (turn a
  one-team win into a repeatable capability).
- **Coach / re-approach** - the numbers improved but adoption is low or decaying: the change is not
  sticking. Find out why (owner, training, the "why" was not sold) before declaring victory.
- **Sunset** - not adopted and not reusable: stop investing and retire it cleanly.

The point is that one measurement discipline serves both the working team (the granular, auditable log)
and leadership (this at-a-glance view) - and the recommendation column is evidence-based, not asserted.
